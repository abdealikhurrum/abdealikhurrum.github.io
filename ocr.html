Lisān OCR

Read Lisān ud-Daʿwat pages into editable text, in your browser.

Upload a scan or photo of a Lisān ud-Daʿwat page — typically set in FatemiMaqala or Kanz al-Marjaan — and this tool recognises the text and drops it into an editable box rendered in FatemiMaqala. Everything runs locally in your browser; nothing is uploaded to a server.

Baseline model: this demo uses Tesseract's stock Arabic model, which reads letters well but drops or mangles the iʿrāb (vowel marks) and the extended Urdu/Persian letters that Lisān ud-Daʿwat depends on. It is wired so a custom fatemi.traineddata — trained on text rendered in these exact fonts — can be dropped in to replace it. Treat the output as a draft to correct, not a finished transcription.

How it works & where it's headed

  • Now: in-browser recognition via Tesseract.js (WASM), no backend.
  • Next: a custom recognizer trained on synthetic lines — Unicode Lisān ud-Daʿwat text rendered in FatemiMaqala and Kanz al-Marjaan with scan-like augmentation — to capture the iʿrāb and extended letters that stock Arabic OCR misses.
  • Then: page layout detection for multi-line scans, and a correction loop that turns your edits into real-world training data.
